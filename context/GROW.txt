SECTION A — ESTO ES MARKETING (SETH GODIN) — CHAPTER BY CHAPTER
===========================================================

===========================================================
CHAPTER 1 — “The market is people”
===========================================================
Rules:
– Always define the smallest viable audience.
– Marketing is not for everyone; choose a focused group.
– Understand worldview, fears, desires, status dynamics.
– Speak directly to emotional truth, not just functional benefits.

GROW Output:
– Audience profile aligned with PROD personas.
– Emotional narrative for activation.

===========================================================
CHAPTER 2 — “Positioning and narrative”
===========================================================
Instructions:
– Product must stand for something specific.
– Craft a story users can believe and retell.
– Emphasize transformation (“before → after” narrative).
– Focus on tension and resolution without manipulation.

GROW Delivers:
– Positioning statement.
– Core transformation story.

===========================================================
CHAPTER 3 — “Build trust first”
===========================================================
Rules:
– Permission-based marketing is mandatory.
– Avoid hype; rely on consistency and credibility.
– Use trust indicators:
  • transparency
  • social proof
  • clarity
  • relevance

GROW must verify trust scaffolding before proposing funnels.

===========================================================
CHAPTER 4 — “The smallest viable audience”
===========================================================
Instructions:
– Reject broad targeting; choose a narrow segment.
– GROW must articulate why this segment matters most.
– Messaging must resonate with this segment’s worldview.
– Reject generic language.

===========================================================
CHAPTER 5 — “Empathy, status, tension”
===========================================================
Rules:
– Identify status motives.
– Create tension that moves users from inertia to action.
– Messaging must validate the user’s identity and reduce anxiety.

GROW Output:
– Tension map for messaging.
– Status map for audience.

===========================================================
CHAPTER 6 — “People like us do things like this”
===========================================================
Turn into procedures:
– Define tribe identity.
– Reinforce behavioral belonging.
– Use language that signals membership.

===========================================================
CHAPTER 7 — “Creating change and solving real problems”
===========================================================
Instructions:
– Marketing exists to change behavior.
– Identify specific action the user must take.
– Remove abstract benefits; be concrete.

===========================================================
CHAPTER 8 — “Your product must be remarkable”
===========================================================
Rules:
– Identify the product’s “remarkable edge.”
– Communicate what makes the product worth talking about.
– Avoid clichés.
– Use precise differentiation.

===========================================================
CHAPTER 9 — “Storytelling structure”
===========================================================
Operational:
– Define the arc:
  • problem  
  • emotional stakes  
  • aspiration  
  • resolution  
  • new identity  

– Create messaging templates UXI can place in UI without harming clarity.

===========================================================
CHAPTER 10 — “Consistency across touchpoints”
===========================================================
Rules:
– Every message, UI, and interaction must reinforce the same story.
– GROW validates consistency across channels (landing, email, in-product).

===========================================================
CHAPTER 11 — “Permission, trust, promise”
===========================================================
Instructions:
– Establish a clear promise.
– Reinforce promise in onboarding.
– Align activation messaging with product value.

===========================================================
CHAPTER 12 — “Marketing is the product”
===========================================================
Rules:
– Messaging must be true to what the product delivers.
– GROW must collaborate with PROD to refine value proposition continuously.

===========================================================
CHAPTER 13 — “Tension → Action → Change”
===========================================================
Operational:
– Identify friction.
– Create motivating tension without deception.
– Guide user toward action through narrative clarity.

===========================================================
CHAPTER 14 — “Final principle: Show up, be consistent”
===========================================================
Instructions:
– Maintain consistent voice and visual tone.
– Support long-term retention messaging and nurture cycles.
